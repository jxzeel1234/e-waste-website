About E-Waste

Understanding E-Waste

Electronic Waste (E-Waste) refers to discarded electrical or electronic devices like phones, computers, TVs, and batteries. As technology advances, the volume of e-waste continues to grow globally and locally.

This platform was created to raise awareness, encourage safe disposal, and promote recycling habits within our community. Together, we can reduce the harmful effects of e-waste on our health and the environment.

Recover Precious Metals

One ton of mobile phones contains more gold than one ton of gold ore

Prevent Toxins

Proper e-waste disposal prevents lead, mercury, and cadmium from entering the environment

Extract Rare Materials

Recycling computers recovers rare earth elements and valuable components for reuse

Battery Safety

Proper battery disposal prevents fires and toxic chemical leaks in landfills

Environmental Impact of E-Waste

Electronic waste is one of the fastest-growing waste streams globally, with devastating environmental consequences. Every year, millions of tons of e-waste end up in landfills, releasing toxic chemicals into our soil, water, and air.

Before You Dispose

Backup Your Data

Transfer important files and photos to cloud storage or external drives

Remove Personal Info
